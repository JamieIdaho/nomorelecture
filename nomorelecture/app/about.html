About Me

"The role of the teacher is one of directing activity rather than actually teaching." - Terry Malloy

Mike Gamblin has been a salesman all his life and as such has a unique insight into what it takes to make it in the real estate business. He grew up in Idaho and Washington, where his father was a General Motors dealer. Mike grew up sweeping the floors as a kid, lot boy as a teen and eventually a salesman, fleet manager, used car manager and finally general manager of one of the dealerships. Mike was the Passenger Car Salesperson of the Year for Chevrolet’s Northwest Region after selling over 300 Chevrolets in a year. Now a broker/owner of his own independent real estate firm, Mike has been a consistent Top Producing real estate agent in the Treasure Valley for the last 22 years. He holds a GRI (Graduate Realtors Institute) and the CRS (Certified Residential Specialist) designations. He has also been instructing real estate agents since 1998.

An avid believer in staying involved in your profession, Mike has served as the president of the Ada County Association of REALTORS in 1998 and was the 2004 State REALTOR President. In 1998 Mike was the Board Forum Chairman for the National Association of REALTORS where local REALTOR boards would meet 3 times a year to discuss issues affecting them. Currently Mike is chairman of the Idaho Real Estate Education Council, a division of the Idaho Real Estate Commission in charge of monitoring and developing real estate curriculum for Idaho’s real estate agents and brokers.

First licensed in 1990, Mike opened Mike Gamblin Real Estate in 1992, a firm specializing in residential brokerage in the Treasure Valley. Mike still remains the designated broker of the firm with approximately 15 agents.

In 2004 Mike opened the Idaho Real Estate School and by 2008 was Idaho’s largest real estate school. Today the Idaho Real Estate School instructs new agents entering the business, as well as continuing education and Broker pre-license courses around the state. Mike was born in Sandpoint, Idaho and now resides in Hidden Springs with his wife Carla. They have four children and three grandchildren.
